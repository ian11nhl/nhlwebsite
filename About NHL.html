What is the NHL?

The NHL is a sports leauge where teams play games against each other to win a trophy called the Stanley Cup. The pre season starts mid-September, and finishes near the first week of October. During the pre season, most players don't play at their best and some players don't even play, because players don't want to get injured before the regular season begins. The regular season begins in the first week of October, and finishes mid-April. The regular season determines which teams will make it to the playoffs based on their points. The playoffs begins mid-April, has four rounds, best of 7 and finishes around June.

Teams

The NHL consists of 32 teams with 7 teams situated in Canada, and 25 teams situated in the USA. The teams are split into two confrences, one being called the Eastern Confrence and the other being called the Western Confrence. The Eastern Confrence consists of two divisions, one being the Metropolian Divison, and the other called the Atlantic Divison, while the Western Confrence consists of two divisions, one being the Pacific Divison and the other called the Central Divison.

All Star Games

All star games are a bonus part of the season where the best players from the East and West play among each other. Last year they replaced all star games with the Four Nations Faceoff. The Four Nations Faceoff starts at the same time as the All Star Games, which starts early February. In the Four Nations Faceoff, there are four teams. The four teams were Canada, USA, Sweden, and Finland. Last year, the finals were between Canada, and USA, and Canada won the cup.



NHL Playoffs

The NHL Stanley Cup Playoffs are the postseason tournament that determines the Stanley Cup Champion. They begin in April after the regular season and usually run until June. Sixteen teams qualify â€” eight from the Eastern Conference and eight from the Western Conference. The top three teams in each division automatically make the playoffs based on regular-season points, while the next two teams with the most points in each conference earn the Wild Card spots. This creates eight playoff teams per conference. In the first round, the division winner with the most points plays the lower Wild Card team, the other division winner faces the higher Wild Card team, and the second- and third-place teams in each division play each other. Each playoff series is a best-of-seven, meaning the first team to win four games advances to the next round. The playoffs progress through four rounds: the First Round, Second Round, Conference Finals, and finally, the Stanley Cup Final, where the Eastern and Western Conference champions compete for the championship. Unlike the regular season, playoff games cannot end in a tie; instead, teams play 20-minute sudden-death overtime periods until one team scores, no matter how long it takes.
